"Born A Crime "

By Trevor Noah

“Identity in Between”

The title describes Trevor's struggle with his mixed heritage and the societal constraints imposed on him
while highlighting his quest for self-definition.

"Abel wanted a traditional marriage with a traditional wife… but he never fell in love with subservient women… 'He only wants a
woman who is free because his dream is to put her in a cage.'”

Personal Reflection:

The quote from a “Born a Crime”,which discusses Abel’s desire for a traditional marriage yet his contradiction in seeking a free woman to confine,resonates deeply with contemporary discussions around gender roles and relationship expectations.It reflects a contradict prevalent in many modern relationships where traditional ideals clash with the evolving understanding of partnership and equality,not because he respected women's freedom, but because he saw them as more easily dominated.Also, Abel's desire to control others is a manifestation of his own lack of self-control, as suggested in the book. He sees women as opportunities to exert his authority and create a "cage" of dependency.

Nowadays,there is often an expectation for women to express independence and excellence.Many men express a desire for strong,independent partners but at the same time uphold traditional gender norms that limit women’s autonomy.This leads to relationships where one partner feels forced despite the outward appearance of freedom.

Real-life reflection

In my own circle,I have seen relationships where men celebrate their partners’ achievements but struggle with the idea of their partner being more successful than they are.The desire for equality creates tension in relationships.Just because a man sees and praises your amazing talent doesn't mean he's the one. He should be your biggest fan, helping you to succeed to new heights.Obviously, also kicking his own life goals and not expecting you to be the breadwinner,cleaner,cook,primary parent, etc.

The rise of discussions around toxic manliness sheds light on how societal pressures can distort men’s perceptions of love and partnership.Many men are conditioned to view vulnerability as weakness,leading them to seek partners who fit into their preconceived notions of femininity rather than embracing mutual respect and partnership.

Misunderstandings create distance.Threfore,having respect for eachother and valuing one's presence helps to connect people or partners leaving ego.
